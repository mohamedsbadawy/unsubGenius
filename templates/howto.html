IMAP (Internet Message Access Protocol) allows you to access your email from third-party apps while keeping everything synced across devices. Many email providers require enabling IMAP and generating app-specific passwords for secure access. Here's how you can set this up for popular email providers.

What is IMAP?

IMAP allows you to access your email across devices while keeping them synchronized. It ensures emails are not downloaded locally but remain on the server.

What is an App-Specific Password?

An app-specific password is a unique code generated by your email provider for secure access to third-party applications. It bypasses two-factor authentication but ensures your account remains safe.

How to Enable IMAP and Generate App-Specific Passwords

Follow these instructions for each provider:

1. Gmail

  • Enable IMAP:
    • Go to mail.google.com.
    • Click the gear icon → See all settings.
    • Under Forwarding and POP/IMAP, enable IMAP and save changes.
  • Generate App-Specific Password:
    • Go to Google Security Settings.
    • Enable 2-Step Verification.
    • Under App Passwords, generate and use the password in your app.

2. Outlook (Hotmail)

  • Enable IMAP:
    • Log in to Outlook.com.
    • Go to Settings → View all Outlook settings.
    • Enable IMAP under Sync Email.
  • Generate App-Specific Password:
    • Go to Microsoft Security.
    • Enable 2-Step Verification.
    • Generate an app password under App Passwords.

3. Yahoo

  • Enable IMAP:
    • Log in to Yahoo Mail.
    • Go to Account Info → Account Security.
    • Enable IMAP or apps with less secure sign-in.
  • Generate App-Specific Password:
    • Go to Yahoo Security.
    • Enable 2-Step Verification.
    • Generate an app password and use it in your app.

4. ATT

  • Enable IMAP: Enable IMAP access through your account profile.
  • Generate App-Specific Password: Go to ATT Secure Mail Key.

5. Comcast

  • Enable IMAP: Log in to Comcast Email Settings and enable IMAP.

6. Verizon

  • Enable IMAP: Log in to your Verizon email account and ensure IMAP is enabled in your settings.

7. AOL

  • Enable IMAP: Log in to AOL, then enable IMAP in the account settings.
  • Generate App-Specific Password: Generate one via AOL Account Security.

8. Zoho

  • Enable IMAP: Go to Zoho Mail Settings and enable IMAP.
  • Generate App-Specific Password: Visit Zoho Security to generate one.
